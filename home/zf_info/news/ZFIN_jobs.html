Job Opening

Java Developer, position # 6089 AB

The Zebrafish Information Network (ZFIN), University of Oregon invites professionals experienced in component based Java development and object oriented analysis to apply for the position of Research Assistant. ZFIN is the zebrafish model organism database and as such works towards integrating zebrafish biology with its genome. ZFIN is a web based resource that is used by a worldwide research community.

Specific Duties:

The functions and responsibilities of this position include but are not limited to:


Assist in the design, implementation and evaluation of web based bioinformatic applications.
Integrate, consolidate and interpret zebrafish gene structures, expression patterns and mutant phenotypes.
Integrate the emerging zebrafish whole-genome sequence with existing genetic and biological data in ZFIN.
Enhance support for various biological ontologies.
Participate in usability testing and evaluation of new ZFIN components.
Monitor, validate and load new data.
Attend and present research talks at meetings, seminars and conferences.

Minimum Required Skills:


Proficiency with Perl, Java, JSP, SQL, J2EE or similar programming languages.
Familiarity with tools and techniques of interface design and evaluation.
n-tier architecture
Understanding of relational database systems.
Familiarity with object-oriented design and development
Strong problem solving skills.
Strong written and oral communication skills.
Proven ability to work well in a team environment.

Education and Experience: Bachelor's degree with three years experience in Computer Science, Mathematics and/or Biological Sciences, Master's preferred.

Scientific Curator

(ZFIN, Institute of Neuroscience, University of Oregon)


ZFIN, the zebrafish model organism database, seeks a scientific curator to join our dynamic, interactive team of biologists and computer scientists at the University of Oregon in Eugene, OR. Scientific curation is emerging as an attractive, alternative career track for creative scientists. As a ZFIN curator you will:


read the latest zebrafish literature, and add information about zebrafish genes, expression patterns and mutant phenotypes to the ZFIN database.
work directly with research laboratories to facilitate the incorporation of their data into ZFIN.
integrate the emerging whole-genome sequence with data available in ZFIN.
collaborate with other bioinformatics professionals to create tools to aid cross-species comparisons.
help design web interfaces to facilitate access to scientific data.

Required:


Ph.D. or M.Sc. degree in the life sciences.
Strong written and verbal communications skills.

Preferred:


Experience in developmental genetics.
Familiarity with biological databases or bioinformatics.
