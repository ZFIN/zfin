Ph.D. Student and Postdoctoral Positions

(GSF-Research Center, Neuherberg, Germany) . Posted: Thu 03 Aug 2000

Back to top

Brain patterning and Neurogenesis in the zebrafish embryo

Ph.D. student and post-doctoral positions are available in the Zebrafish Neurogenetics group of the Technical University, Munich, Germany, to study embryonic brain patterning and neurogenesis in vertebrates.

Our main model system is the embryo of the zebrafish, Danio rerio, with the expertise to turn to the avian system when more appropriate. We primarily focus on the development of the zebrafish mid-hindbrain territory, with possible extensions to more anterior brain domains. Projects will include the functional analysis of new genes expressed within the presumptive mid-hindbrain, the establishment of new mid-hindbrain mutant lines, and analyses of the relationship between anterior endoderm and neural plate patterning. We routinely use non-genetic techniques such as RNA / DNA injections into zebrafish embryos, lineage tracing and transplantations, and have expertise with genetic approaches including transgenesis, mutagenesis and molecular mapping.

The group is located at the GSF-Research Center in Munich / Neuherberg, which posseses a new and highly performant zebrafish facility as well as core facilities for high throughput molecular techniques. The GSF-Research Center hosts several other strong development biology groups focussing on brain or skeletal development.

The applicants should have a strong interest in developmental biology and / or neurogenesis. Previous experience with the zebrafish system is not required. Applicants should send a curriculum vitae by post or e-mail to the address below. In addition, two reference letters should be provided by the post-doctoral applicant.

Technical Assistant position

A technical assistant position is available in the same group, to study neural development using the zebrafish as a model system. Experience in biological techniques such as DNA isolation, probes synthesis or gene expression analyses would be appreciated. In addition, interest in working with the zebrafish system, interactive spirit and good knowledge of the English language are required. The initial contract will be limited to two years and can be extended up to five years. The salary will be according to the German Civil Service regulations.

Please note: For hygiene reasons, the applicant should not have pet fish at home.

Please send your curriculum vitae by post or e-mail to the address below.

Aquatic Biologist Position Opening

(NASA Ames Research Center, Moffett Field, CA USA) . Posted: Thu 17 Aug 2000

Back to top

This is a permanent position that has recently opened up in our organization - Lockheed Martin Engineering and Sciences. It involves supporting a NASA contract for developing an Aquatic Habitat for research in a microgravity environment on the International Space Station.

Required Qualifications

PhD in aquatic biology, aquatic physiology, or related life sciences discipline, with minimum 2-3 years post-doctoral experience, and significant research experience in fish and aquatic biology or physiology. Evidence of ability to conduct independent research and publish data. Excellent comunication skills, both verbal and written. Desired Qualifications

Experience with Excel, Microsoft Word, and Filemaker Pro. Desirable that work experience was in a multidisciplinary and team environment. Ability to supervise technical support for aquatic laboratory research. A diverse background in working with aquatic specimens which are model systems for NASA research programs. Experience with NASA life science flight operations or space programs.

Responsibilities

This individual will be responsible for acting as the central SSBRP science oversight for the development of the Aquatic Habitat (AQH). This responsibility requires staying current on all science issues related to the design, testing, development and validation of the AQH. It will require maintaining professional interfaces with the SSBRP Science Lead, SSBRP Engineering Lead, and interfaces with the hardware developer, the NASA Project Scientist, and the SSBRP Science Working Group. This also requires maintaining a complete understanding of the science requirements for AQH design and ensuring that the vendor develops a complete testing program which validates the science requirements. At times this position will require travel to hardware reviews and aquatic biology research workshops. The job may also require preparing white papers and analyses of data which are needed to support AQH development. The scientist will need to develop a solid database of potential areas for aquatic biology research in space and will at times be involved in providing technical input on proposals which would eventually lead to flight experiments. This position may also require design and conduct of research studies which further refine the understanding of science requirements for the AQH development, and may require publishing or presenting results in research fora.

Postdoctoral & Graduate Student Positions

(Netherlands Ophthalmic Research Institute in Amsterdam) . Posted Thurs 14 Sept 2000

Back to top

Postdoctoral and graduate student positions available immediately!

Research Unit "Retinal Signal Processing" of the Netherlands Ophthalmic Research Institute in Amsterdam in the Netherlands studies the communication between neurons in the retina of the zebrafish and goldfish. The research has a strong emphasis on the functional consequences of retinal processing on for the visual system as a whole. In the laboratory, electrophysiological (patch-clamp, intracellular and multi-electrode), fluorescent-imaging, immunocytochemical, molecular biological and behavioral techniques are used in combination with computer simulations.

For the project "Mechanisms of sustained synaptic transmission" we are looking for a postdoc (4 years) with a molecular biological training and a postdoc/graduate student (4 years) with an electrophysiological background. From both candidates we expect a strong affinity with functional studies.

The project aims to unravel the mechanisms of sustained synaptic transmission in the cone/horizontal/bipolar cell synapse in the retina of the goldfish and zebrafish. In this project we want to identify the key proteins involved in the synaptic machinery of the cone photoreceptors and study their role in synaptic transmission functionally. That part of the project deals with the relation between the Ca-current, the Ca-concentration and the synaptic vesicle release by the cone photoreceptors. Two approaches will followed: a molecular biological and an electrophysiological/fluorescent-imaging approach. In the second part of the project we would like to apply transgenic techniques on zebrafish to interfere with the synaptic machinery.
