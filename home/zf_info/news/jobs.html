Postdoctoral Position

(Center for Molecular Neurobiology - University of Hamburg, Germany)


A post-doctoral positions in zebrafish neurobiology is available at the Center for Molecular Neurobiology at the University Hamburg to study cell adhesion molecules in nervous system development, regeneration and synaptic plasticity.

Our previous work (for reviews see, for instance, Dityatev and Schachner, Nat. Rev. Neurosci. 4, 456-468 (2003); Kleene and Schachner, Nat. Rev. Neurosci. 5, 195-208 (2004) and http://www.zmnh.uni-hamburg.de) demonstrated that cell interactions depend on the finely tuned interplay of recognition molecules at the cell surface and in the extracellular matrix and that not only the protein backbone, but also glycans attached to the protein backbone are required for function via signal transduction.

Work in this area has led to the use of zebrafish and analyses of cell interactions during development and regeneration. Recent experiments show that recognition molecules, in particular L1, are beneficial for development and in enhancing regenerative processes in spinal cord lesion paradigms in zebrafish and mice. Furthermore, peptidomimetics and small organic compounds for carbohydrates and functionally important epitopes on L1 have been and are being developed that are presently tested in mouse and zebrafish models of neurodegeneration. Publications of the group are among the 100 most cited ones in the Neurosciences worldwide according to ISI as monitored in 2000-2003.

We are looking for enthusiastic and co-operative scientists with experience in working with zebrafish, for instance during development using methods in molecular biology, biochemistry, tissue culture, and/or animal surgery to engage in a multi-disciplinary approach with the goal to apply our knowledge in recognition molecule function in a very powerful animal model system, namely the zebrafish, to therapy in neurodegenerative diseases in humans. Send applications to Melitta Schachner at: melitta.schachner@zmnh.uni-hamburg.de

Postdoctoral Research Positions in Neuronal Regeneration

(University of Notre Dame)


Postdoctoral Research Positions in Neuronal Regeneration

One or two postdoctoral research positions are available to participate in the study of neuronal regeneration of the damaged zebrafish retina at the University of Notre Dame. We are using several different paradigms to cause the loss of different neuronal populations, including the light-induced loss of rod and cone photoreceptors (Vihtelic and Hyde (2000) J. Neurobiol. 44: 289-307). Gene microarray experiments have identified a number of candidate genes that are involved in specific stages of the regeneration response and additional microarray experiments are planned to identify candidates that are required for the regeneration of specific neuronal cell types. Genetic tools, such as the Cre-lox and Tet-on systems, are being developed to drive cell-specific and inducible expression of specific transgenes to analyze the regeneration response.

The lab consists of several postdocs and graduate students. Two technicians maintain the feeding and husbandry of the large zebrafish facility (approximately 3,500 tanks) daily. The lab is well-funded by both external grants and money provided by the University.

We are looking for talented individuals who may have experience in molecular biology, genetics, cell biology, genomics, biochemistry, developmental biology or related fields. Previous experience with zebrafish is desirable, but not mandatory. More experienced individuals may be considered to be hired at the non-tenure track Research Assistant Professor level. The successful candidate may start at any time.
