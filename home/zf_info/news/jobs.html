Ph.D. Student and Technical Assistant Positions

(Institute of Developmental Genetics - Munich, Germany)


Ph.D. Student position
Brain patterning and Neurogenesis in the zebrafish embryo

A Ph.D. student position is available in the Zebrafish Neurogenetics group of the Technical University, Munich, Germany, to study embryonic brain patterning and neurogenesis in vertebrates.

Our main model system is the embryo of the zebrafish, Danio rerio. We primarily focus on the midbrain-hindbrain territory, where neurogenesis control permits the long-lasting maintenance of undifferentiated cells at the midbrain-hindbrain boundary (MHB). These cells are important for midbrain-hindbrain growth and diversification throughout life, and can serve as a model for the study of vertebrate neuronal progenitors.

We recently identified two crucial factors involved in the formation and maintenance of these progenitors (Geling et al., Development, 2003; Geling et al., Development, 2004; Ninkovic et al., Development, in press) and we now aim at analyzing the molecular and cellular cascades involving these factors. Projects will include isolating and testing the function of new genes expressed within the presumptive midbrain-hindbrain progenitor pool, studying midbrain-hindbrain regulatory elements in transgenic lines, and analysing the integration of patterning signals and neurogenesis control at the MHB. We routinely use non-genetic techniques such as RNA / DNA injections into zebrafish embryos, lineage tracing and transplantations, and have expertise with genetic approaches including transgenesis, mutagenesis and molecular mapping.

The group is located at the GSF-Research Centre in Munich / Neuherberg, with a high-performance zebrafish facility and core facilities for high throughput molecular techniques. The GSF-Research Centre hosts several strong development biology groups focussing on brain or skeletal development in the mouse, chicken or zebrafish.

The applicants should have a strong interest in developmental biology and / or neurogenesis. Previous experience with the zebrafish system is not required. Applicants should send a curriculum vitae by post or e-mail to the address below. Whenever possible, two reference letters should be provided.

The applicant should not have pet fish at home.
