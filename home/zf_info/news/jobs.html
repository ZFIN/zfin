Postdoctoral Position in Assembly of the Muscle Sarcomere

(King's College London)

A postdoctoral position is available in the Simon Hughes lab to study sarcomere and myofibril assembly in skeletal muscle in zebrafish. The project provides a unique opportunity to study a fundamental cell biological process, the building of a complex cytoskeletal multiprotein machine, in a temporally defined and readily accessible in vivo situation. We will unify genome wide approaches with in vivo imaging to understand the order of events during sarcomere assembly, a process fundamental to animal evolution.

Hinits, Y. and S.M. Hughes (2007) Mef2s are required for thick filament formation in nascent muscle fibers. Development. 134: 2511-2519.

The Hughes lab is based at King's College London in the major Guy's biomedical campus of the largest medical school in Europe in the heart of the metropolis. The Randall Division specialises in the development of new biophysical and imaging approaches to the study of muscle and other tissues. The campus contains about 15 zebrafish groups and 20 labs working on muscle.

Applicants must be enthusiastic and highly motivated with experience in cell and molecular biology. Zebrafish and advantage but not essential. Send a CV with publications and references to:

The overall aim is to understand how these synapses transfer information contained within a visual stimulus.

This technique allows the visualization of synaptic vesicles and fluorescent molecules within about 100 nm of the surface membrane. We have generated fish that express fluorescent fusion proteins regulating vesicle behaviour at or near the active zone and are using these to monitor the vesicle cycle in real-time.
