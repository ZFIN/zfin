Postdoctoral Fellow and Research Assistant

(University College Dublin - Dublin, Ireland)


Applications are invited for a Postdoctoral Fellow (Ph.D.) and a Research Assistant (B.Sc./M.Sc. level) position available early in 2006. Our lab is situated within the UCD Conway Institute which provides state-of-the-art facilites for genomics, proteomics, and microscopy (http://www.ucd.ie/conway/).

Zebrafish (Danio rerio), small tropical fish, have emerged as a prolific, vertebrate model for genetic and developmental studies. We apply the zebrafish model to investigate cone photoreceptor development and disease. Cone photoreceptors are critical for sharp, colour vision in daylight and loss of cone photoreceptors leads to severe blindness.

Our interests are to identify key regulators that direct cone photoreceptor development and differentiation, and to identify therapeutic targets for cone disease. Our approaches include genetic screens, transgenesis, fluorescent-activated cell sorting and microarray analysis.

DanioLabs is a Cambridge (UK) based therapeutics company that uses phenotype driven drug discovery technologies to identify novel treatments for a variety of human diseases. A major part of the DanioLabs' platform involves the use of zebrafish as an experimental species. As a result of recent successes, DanioLabs is expanding its operations and is looking to recruit an outstanding post-doctoral researcher to join our program in the identification of therapeutics for multiple sclerosis. DanioLabs strategy focuses on identifying molecules which promote remyelination of the nervous system and utilises proprietary high throughput in vivo models in larval zebrafish, coupled with systematic, compound screens. The zebrafish work integrates into a wider program of therapy identification in multiple sclerosis encompassing inter-linked basic science and clinical work in the Cambridge area.
