Research Assistant Position

(The Rockefeller University) . Posted 27 Jan 2003

Back to top

A multidisciplinary research group that studies the cellular and molecular basis of hearing and balance is looking for a technician to run a state-of-the-art zebrafish facility with 1,200 tanks. The responsibilities will include the breeding and rearing of zebrafish, maintaining the facility, assisting with genetic studies, and training group members in aquaculture.

Skills required

The successful applicant must have a bachelor's degree in the biological sciences, preferably in marine biology, animal science, or aquaculture. Reliability, motivation, and strong organizational skills are necessary; prior experience with zebrafish husbandry would be a plus.

Research environment

The Hudspeth research group is investigating how hair cells, the sensory receptors of the internal ear, transduce mechanical inputs, such as sounds and accelerations, into electrical signals to the brain. Members of the group are employing anatomical, behavioral, biochemical, biophysical, electrophysiological, embryological, genetic, and molecular-biological techniques. The recent development of zebrafish as an experimental model system offers a unique opportunity to investigate the genetic basis of hearing disorders, which affect nearly one in ten Americans. The applicant will participate in an ongoing genetic screen of zebrafish for mutations that affect the ear and the lateral-line organ. All members of the group meet weekly to discuss research plans and results and to review recent findings from other laboratories.

Postdoctoral Position

(IMCB, Singapore) . Posted 18 Mar 2003

Back to top

One Postdoctoral Position to study zebrafish liver organogenesis is available immediately in Jinrong Peng's lab at The Institute of Molecular and cell Biology (IMCB), Singapore. IMCB is a leading research institute in Asia and is fully funded by Singapore government (http://www.imcb.nus.edu.sg/).

Our goal is to identify genes involved in liver initiation and development in zebrafish. We have initiated a moderate scale of screening for liver defect mutants and ~50 putative mutants have been isolated. We have just started to clone these mutant genes via positional cloning method in hope to reveal how these genes are involved in controlling liver development. In addition, our lab has obtained ~15,000 unique cDNA clusters via our EST sequencing project. At the same time, we have setup microarray facility with optimized methods and conditions (Lo et al, 2003, Genome Research, pp455-466). We believe that the combination of genomic and genetic approaches will greatly facilitate our research program.

The successful applicant will be enthusiastic about developmental biology and have training in molecular techniques, embryology, or preferably both - ideally with some zebrafish experience. Recent PhD. graduates, or those close to graduation, are encouraged to apply.

For more information please see our WEB page or contact Jinrong Peng by e-mail. To apply for the position please send a CV and outline of your background and interests, plus the names of three referees qualified to comment on your suitability for this position to:

Jinrong Peng
pengjr@imcb.nus.edu.sg

Postdoctoral Position

(University of Wisconsin, Madison) . Posted 23 May 2003

Back to top
  

One NIH-funded postdoctoral position is available to the study the function of the gene aura during the early cellular cleavages, as well as molecularly clone the gene. A recessive maternal-effect mutation in this gene affects late cytokinesis and the recruitment of germ plasm to the furrow. Our preliminary analysis suggests that this gene has a role in cytoskeletal rearrangements in the early embryo, possibly of the microfilament network, which in turn are required for vesicle secretion and germ plasm recruitment during furrow formation. We use the analysis of mutant and wild-type embryos in order to understand at the cellular and molecular level the mechanisms required for these processes.

We are located in Madison, Wisconsin, a city that is consistently rated as one of the most livable cities in the U.S. The biomedical community at the University of Wisconsin - Madison constitutes a superb scientific environment in a collegiate atmosphere. Applicants should have experience in Developmental and/or Cell Biology and familiarity with molecular techniques.

For more information, please contact Francisco Pelegri at fjpelegri@wisc.edu

Position-Nr. 22/2003 at UFZ Centre for Environmental Research Leipzig-Halle, Germany

Leipzig, 23.05.03

The UFZ-Centre for Environmental Research Leipzig-Halle located in Leipzig, Halle and Magdeburg is a member of the Hermann von Helmholtz-Association.

The Junior Research Group of Molecular Animal Cell Toxicology at the UFZ Centre for Environmental Research Leipzig-Halle will fill a position of a

Doctoral fellow

starting October 01, 2003. The position is funded by the BMBF (German Federal Ministry for Education and Science, research program for 'Alternatives to testing laboratory animals/biotechnology'), is limited to a period of 3 years and offers the possibility to earn a Ph.D. degree (German dissertation).

Toxic effects of environmental chemicals can directly lead to the alteration of the regulation of target genes. Gene expression patterns may also change in response to the cellular status modified by a toxic compound. The aim of the project is to identify xenobiotic-sensitive genes in the zebrafish embryo. Gene expression patterns will be analysed in order to replace chronic and prolonged fish tests. The project is performed in strong cooperation with the ECT Oekotoxikologie GmbH, Flörsheim, Germany and the Institute of Hydrobiology, Group of Ecotoxicology, TU Dresden, Germany. Therefore, the applicant is expected to have appropriate team work skills.

The candidate should have an degree in biology, biochemistry, biotechnology or any related field. We also assume experiences in molecular biology. The candidate should be interested in research relevant to the environment, toxicology and developmental biology. The research of the JRG Molecular Animal Cell Toxicology is focussed on molecular ecotoxicology and technologies to rapidly identify environmental contaminants based on their biological effects.  Current projects include the analysis of the molecular effects of endocrine disrupting chemicals and the development of a cDNA array.

The place of work is Leipzig.
Salary will be according to the appropriate civil service level (BAT-O IIa/2).
