    Postdoctoral Position

    (Leibniz Institute for Age Research, Fritz-Lipmann-Institute, Jena, Germany )

    The "Biology of Ageing" Laboratory (A. Cellerino, SNS Pisa, Italy) at the Leibniz Institute for Age Research, Fritz-Lipmann-Institute, Jena, Germany is inviting applications for a Postdoctoral position (TV-L E13)

    Identification of physiological and molecular mechanisms controlling aging in the short-lived fish Nothobranchius furzeri

    Investigations into ageing in vertebrates is hampered by the absence of animals models with very short life span. In addition, very little information is available as to the genes controlling ageing and age-related dysfunctions in natural populations, due to lack of model systems where differences in aging phenotypes among natural populations are well-described. In the last years, we have established the annual fish Nothobranchius furzeri as a new model system for age research. The captive life span of this fish is only few months and it shows a greatly accelerated expression of typical age-related markers and these can be modulated by treatments known to increase lifespan in other model organisms. Populations of the short-lived fish Nothobranchius furzeri show large differences in lifespan and onset of age-related pathologies. Our main areas of research are: (1) Testing of life-extending interventions, (2) Identification of biological/molecular differences which correlate with differences in natural lifespan. Projects are conducted in strict collaboration with the groups Molecular Biology (Englert) and Genome Analysis (Platzer) within a "Nothobranchius project" at FLI.

    More informations: http://www.fli-leibniz.de/groups/cellerino_en.php

    Further questions: acellerino@fli-leibniz.de

    We are looking for a candidate with a strong background in physiology/anatomy/pathology OR molecular biology OR evolutionary biology/behavioural studies with strong team capability, who wishes to contribute to a multidisciplinary project. Previous experience with fish model systems is a plus, but not a prerequisite.

    PhD Student Position

    (Max Planck Institute for Medical Research, Heidelberg, Germany)

    Marie Curie PhD Fellowship is available at the laboratory of Dr. Soojin Ryu at the Max Planck Institute for Medical Research in Heidelberg, Germany to join our work on investigating development and function of neuronal circuits in vertebrate central nervous system (http://circuits.mpimf-heidelberg.mpg.de). The project will involve analyzing the formation and function of neuronal circuits that mediate the stress response in zebrafish. The stress response is crucial for homeostasis and the survival of an animal, yet chronic stress is detrimental and underlies many pathological conditions such as depression. The goal of this project is to determine the effect of chronic stress on neural circuit formation and function in vivo utilizing transgenic zebrafish lines that express various genetically encoded sensors in cell type specific manner. Circuit formation will be analyzed at single-cell resolution using in vivo time-lapse imaging. Circuit function will be analyzed by combining genetic, optogenetic and pharmacological manipulation with calcium imaging and behavioral analysis.

    Interested candidate should have a strong background in neuroscience and practical experiences in microscopy. The position is funded by Marie Curie Fellowship, which offers a very generous stipend, travel and relocation fund.

    The Max Planck Institute for Medical Research provides a multi-disciplinary and interactive scientific environment with a state-of-art imaging facility and a newly built fish facility. The start date is somewhat flexible but should be during the first half of 2010.
