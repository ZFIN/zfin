Postdoctoral Position

(Pasteur Institute, Paris) . Posted 18 Oct 2002

Back to top

A postdoctoral position is available at the Pasteur Institute in Paris, France, to study hematopoiesis in the zebrafish embryo, notably (but not only) the early lineage of primitive macrophages (Development 126 : 3735; Dev. Biol. 238 : 274, and webpage indicated below), their roles in the embryo and in the development of the immune system, and their fate in the adult. The potential projects involve:

  • high-resolution in vivo imaging of cell behaviours (of leukocytes and hemopoietic precursors), including intracellular events, in embryonic and later tissues - combining video-enhanced DIC microscopy with appropriately designed transgenic GFP zebrafish lines, and/or cell transplantation;
  • laser-mediated in situ activation of fluorescent cell tracers;
  • pharmacological or oligonucleotide-based suppression-of-function;
  • injection of pathogenic/non-pathogenic microorganisms;
  • a phenotypic screen for mutants defective in macrophage differentiation or migration into tissues.

Training in molecular and/or zebrafish developmental biology will be appreciated but is not mandatory. The Pasteur Institute features over 1000 full-time researchers on a pleasant campus in the centre of Paris, with several new "technological platforms" (central facilities), including a powerful and quite friendly "Dynamic Imaging Centre".

The facilities and instrumentation are state-of-the-art. Konstanz is located on Lake Constance and is close to the Swiss Alps and Zürich airport.
