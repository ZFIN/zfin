    Postdoctoral Research Associate

    Academic Neurology Unit, Division of Genomic Medicine, Centre of Developmental and Biomedical Genetics (CDBG)


    Ref: R3897 Closing Date: 25/05/06

    Mutations in LRRK2 are the most common genetic cause for Parkinson's disease (PD). The function of LRRK2 is still poorly understood. The zebrafish is a new and promising animal model for human diseases. The aim of this project is to characterize the zebrafish homologue of human LRRK2 and determine its expression in early development. The effect of LRRK2 knockdown on neuronal survival will be established and zebrafish with mutations in LRRK2 from an already undertaken ENU mutagenesis screen will be identified. This will facilitate future studies using stable LRRK2 mutant zebrafish lines for drug/compound screens.

    The successful candidate will work with a postdoctoral scientist, a PhD student and a technician, all of whom use zebrafish as a model for neurodegeneration. S/he will work in the highly successful laboratory of Professor Phillip Ingham, international authority in zebrafish research, at the Centre of Developmental and Biomedical Genetics (CDBG). Professor Ingham is an international authority in zebrafish research and has established several collaborations with clinicians so that there are now several groups at the CDBG which use zebrafish as an animal model for human diseases. Candidates should have a PhD or equivalent in a biological science, or be working towards a PhD. Previous experience with zebrafish research is essential. The post is available from 1 July 2006 for a period of 12 months, with the possibility of renewal subject to funding.

    Further details on: http://www.sheffield.ac.uk/jobs/research.html

    The successful candidate has international reputation in stem cell research, incl. embryonic stem cells, and conducts a vigorous basic research program to understand the genomic basis of regeneration and stem cell activity using model organisms.
