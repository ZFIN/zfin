Postdoctoral Position in Cell & Developmental Biology

(Université of Marseille, Luminy) . Posted 12 Jun 2002

Back to top

Applications are encouraged to a European Union-funded two-years post-doctoral position to study the coupling between fate specification and cell division in the developing central nervous system of the ascidian Ciona intestinalis. This model system is currently becoming a reference model organism due to the simplicity of its embryos, the existence of an invariant lineage, the recent sequencing of its genome and the efficiency of morpholino-based gene knock-down experiments. Most importantly, it allows the description of developmental processes with a single-cell level of resolution.

Depending on the background and interests of applicants, several projects involving a combination of embryological, cellular, molecular and bio-computing approaches can be discussed.

Our group is located at the IBDM, on the Luminy Campus in Marseilles, France. The Institute hosts around 30 groups working in the field of Developmental Biology on a variety of vertebrate and invertebrate systems. It provides a friendly and interactive environment as well as state of the art core facilities in imaging and genomics.

Applicants familiar with molecular approaches and with a background in Developmental or Cell Biology, should e-mail a CV and a brief description of their current research interests to:

Patrick Lemaire
