Conference on "Aquatic Animal Models of Human Disease"

September 29-October 2, 2003, American Type Culture Collection (ATCC), Manassas, VA USA

Back to top

The University of Miami and the American Type Culture Collection (ATCC) are pleased to announce a conference entitled "Aquatic Animal Models of Human Disease." This meeting is sponsored by the National Center for Research Resources (NCRR) and the National Cancer Institute (NCI) of the National Institutes of Health and will be held at the ATCC facility in Manassas, VA, from September 29 through October 2, 2003. Topics for scientific sessions will include the use of aquatic animal models for the study of comparative genomics, gene expression, transgenesis, carcinogenesis, toxicology, infectious disease, neurological disorders and aging. Workshops are planned on transgenesis and gene expression, as well as on current technologies for resource development and funding mechanisms. Submissions of abstracts for oral and poster presentations are invited with a deadline of July 1, 2003. Registration will be limited. For complete details, please see the conference website: http://pasteur.atcc.org/aquatic_conference/

6th Australia & New Zealand Zebrafish Workshop

February 8-10, 2004, Victor Harbour (near Adelaide) Australia

Back to top

Dear Zebrafish Researchers,

I would like to invite you to the 6th Australia and New Zealand Zebrafish Workshop to be held at Victor Harbour (near Adelaide) Australia from 8 to 10 February in 2004. Information on the workshop, the resort venue, and a registration form etc. can be found at the following website, http://www.cmgd.adelaide.edu.au/conference/index.html 
http://www.cmgd.adelaide.edu.au/conference/RegistrationForm.pdf

The workshop is held conveniently one week before the excellent Lorne Genome Conference (16 - 20 February 2004 near Melbourne, see http://www.genome-conf.net.au/lorne/). One can drive from the Victor Harbour to Lorne along one of the world's most beautiful highways, The Great Ocean Road (see http://www.greatoceanrd.org.au/).

Note that the registration and accommodation prices for the workshop are in Australian Dollars (AUD, currently equivalent to ~US$0.65) so you should find them very reasonable. Registration is required by 3 October and the abstract deadline (if you wish to make a presentation) is 17 November. Session topics will be determined when all abstracts have been submitted.

We would like to see as many international guests as possible so we hope that you will take this opportunity to contribute to the success of our workshop and to visit "the Antipodes"!
