EMBO World Program Workshop -
"Fish as Model Organisms in the Genomic Era"

October 24-26, 2001, Singapore
Back to top

As part of the new "EMBO World Program", a workshop entitled "Fish as Model Organisms in the Genomic Era" will be held in Singapore, from October 24th to the 26th, 2001. The aim of this workshop is to introduce the zebrafish community to genome-based approaches currently being used in other systems, and to stimulate development of techniques that will make the fish an even better experimental model, especially once more genome sequence data becomes available. Accordingly, as well as talks on various aspects of research using zebrafish, medaka and pufferfish, the workshop will feature speakers working with the other major animal model systems: Xenopus, Drosophila, chick, mouse and C. elegans.

Further information is available from the website (http://embo.ima.org.sg) or from the organisers, Phil Ingham (p.w.ingham@sheffield.ac.uk) and Suresh Jesuthasan (suresh@ima.org.sg).

The deadline for application, which should be made via the website, is 30th June, 2001. We look forward to seeing you in tropical Singapore!
