Short Course for mRNA Caging Technology Using Bhc-diazo

October 29-November 1, 2001, RIKEN Brain Science Institute, Saitama, Japan

Back to top

(hosted by Hideki Ando, Toshiaki Furuta and Hitoshi Okamoto)

We are planning to have a practical short course for mRNA caging technology using Bhc-diazo from Monday, October 29th through Thursday, November 1st. This technology enables activation of any gene at an arbitrary developmental stage and tissue [Ando, H., Furuta, T., Tsien, R. Y. and Okamoto, H. (2001) Photo-mediated gene activation using caged RNA/DNA in zebrafish embryos. Nature Genetics, 28: 317-325. ] Although this technology is very simple in principle, we still think it preferable to demonstrate the detailed procedure directly to the people who are interested in using this technology. Some people who hope to try their own mRNA could stay on for the rest of the week. Because of the limitation of the number of the microscopes and the capacity of the room, we have to limit the number of participants to 15 through 20 people at most. We can provide financial support for accommodation, but other expense including airfare must be paid by yourself. To get financial support from our institute, we may have to ask some of the participants to give a seminar talk. Our campus is located 15 min by train away from the downtown of Tokyo. Access information can be found at http://www.riken.go.jp/engn/r-world/riken/campus/index.html. If you are interested in attending this course, please contact Hitoshi Okamoto (hitoshi@brain.riken.go.jp) by September 15th.

 

EMBO World Program Workshop -
"Fish as Model Organisms in the Genomic Era"

October 24-26, 2001, Singapore
Back to top

As part of the new "EMBO World Program", a workshop entitled "Fish as Model Organisms in the Genomic Era" will be held in Singapore, from October 24th to the 26th, 2001. The aim of this workshop is to introduce the zebrafish community to genome-based approaches currently being used in other systems, and to stimulate development of techniques that will make the fish an even better experimental model, especially once more genome sequence data becomes available. Accordingly, as well as talks on various aspects of research using zebrafish, medaka and pufferfish, the workshop will feature speakers working with the other major animal model systems: Xenopus, Drosophila, chick, mouse and C. elegans.

Further information is available from the website (http://embo.ima.org.sg) or from the organisers, Phil Ingham (p.w.ingham@sheffield.ac.uk) and Suresh Jesuthasan (suresh@ima.org.sg).

The deadline for application, which should be made via the website, is 30th June, 2001. We look forward to seeing you in tropical Singapore!
