EMBO Practical Course on Animal Models for Development and Disease

July 12-24, 2004
The Centre for Developmental Genetics
University of Sheffield, UK


Web pages:
http://www.shef.ac.uk/devgen/embocourse/

The objectives of this course are to teach genetic and molecular embryological techniques for analysing gene function, cell fate and tissue interactions in model organisms such as Drosophila melanogaster, zebrafish, chick and Caenorhabditis elegans. The course is aimed primarily at active researchers in the field of developmental biology and also at more clinically oriented workers seeking to exploit model organisms for the analysis of human disease mechanisms. Students may be senior pre-doctoral as well as post-doctoral and will be selected on the basis of scientific merit and needs. There will be no charge for board and lodging and no course fee. Please see the web pages at the above address for details on how to apply.

Please note the deadline for applications is 16 April 2004.

Fish Genetics and Development Workshop - Chinese Academy of Sciences

October 11-14, 2004
Wuhan,China

The overseas Chinese biologists are invited to present their latest progress in fish genetics and development studies. The workshop will include topics such as fish development, genomics, transgenesis and technology, endocrinology, and immunology. During the workshop, Drs. Perry Hackett, Choy Leong Hew, Yoshitaka Nagahama, Bernard Thisse and Monte Westerfield will give special lectures.

Please check out the websites http://www.ihb.ac.cn/fishdev/index.htm or http://clonfish.myrice.com for guidelines of abstract submission, workshop registration, scientific program, and other related information. (better use a PC browser to view these websites).

Contacting person: Dr. Yong-Hua Sun (Institute of Hydrobiology, Chinese Academy of Sciences)
