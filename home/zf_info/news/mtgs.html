IBC's Model Organisms for Drug Discovery Conference

August 8-9, 2002, Boston, Massachusetts USA
Back to top

Conference Title:       IBC’s Model Organisms for Drug Discovery
Organizer:                   IBC Life Sciences
Conference Dates:   August 8-9, 2002
Location:                     Boston, MA
Venue:                         The Hynes Convention Center
Webs Site:                    http://www.LifeSciencesInfo.com/2713/?source=2713-35

Conference Details:

IBC’s inaugural conference on Model Organisms for Drug Discovery provides not only an up-to-date look at the latest tools, but also case sutides of model organism applications from the end-users. Leading industry companies such as AstraZeneca, Bristol-Myers Squibb and Millennium will discuss target validation using single and multiple model systems. Aventis, Ingenium Pharmaceuticals, Deltagen, and Bristol-Myers Squibb will present single and multiple models to validate targets across multiple target classes, to elucidate mechanisms of drug action and to develop assays for identifying lead compounds. In addition, a number of model system platforms including C.elegans, Drosophila, mice, yeast, and zebrafish will be presented.

2003 Australia-New Zealand Zebrafish Workshop

February 10-11, 2003, Auckland, New Zealand
Back to top

For the past 4 years zebrafish researchers in Australia and New Zealand have held an annual workshop to discuss our research projects. In February, 2003 we are holding the 5th Workshop on Waiheke Island, which is situated in the Hauraki Gulf close by to Auckland.

The format of this 2 day informal meeting allows PIs, postdoctoral fellows and graduate students to present their research, followed by plenty of discussion time. We will begin the Workshop on Sunday February 9 with an evening mixer. The meeting will finish early afternoon February 11. As with previous workshops we invite an overseas investigator to be the keynote speaker and also lead discussions around some experimental issues or procedures. In 2003 Didier Stainier will be this speaker.

The workshop is open to all zebrafish investigators, not just those from Down-Under! Why don't you combine a vacation in New Zealand with some science for 2 days!

Waiheke Island is a fantastic location. February is summertime in New Zealand. The workshop will take place just days before the start of the America's Cup on the Hauraki Gulf. The Workshop venue will be the Waiheke Island Resort where we will also accommodate all attendees and have our meals. Waiheke Island is a short ferry ride from downtown Auckland. The Resort has tennis courts and a swimming pool, as well as being close to a beautiful golden sand beach. Waiheke Island has many activities on offer: ~28 vineyards, cafes, mountain biking, great beaches, golf, fishing, diving, kayaking, sailing, bush-walking, to name just a few!

http://www.waihekeresort.co.nz/home.htm

The costs for the meeting will be modest.

XIX International Congress of Genetics

July 6-12, 2003, Melbourne, Australia
Back to top

Biological research is woven into the fabric of Australia's history.

In 1770 the east coast of Australia was explored as part of a scientific expedition.

The eminent naturalist, Sir Joseph Banks, chronicled the unique flora and fauna of a new continent with his sumptuous illustrations and his elaborate journal. In 1836 Charles Darwin followed the Australian coast in the voyage of the H.M.S. Beagle. In July 2003 a new page in this rich history will turn as elite geneticists from around the world gather en masse for the XIX International Congress of Genetics in Melbourne, Australia. This Congress will address the theme 'Genomes - The Linkage to Life' and celebrates the 50th anniversary of the discovery of the structure of DNA. A total of 280 invited speakers will address over 3000 people in a total of 54 symposia and 9 plenary addresses. A significant number of Zebrafish researchers will be on the program.  Plan your journey to the Congress, explore its theme, Genomes - The Linkage to Life, and discover for yourself the awesome natural wonders of Australia.

The web site at http://www.geneticscongress2003.com/ will provide the latest information on the XIX International Congress of Genetics. At the moment it is a source of basic information on the Congress venue, program and travel in Australia.

You are invited to add your name to the Congress mailing list to ensure that you will be notified of major updates to the information on this site.
