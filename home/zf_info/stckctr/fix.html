Histopathology Sample Preparation. Fish should be fixed prior to shipment using the protocol below. It is best if fish are alive prior to fixation. Dead fish autolyze rapidly and are not good specimens for histopathology. Do not freeze fish and do not use aqueous formalin or paraformaldehyde fixatives. These fixatives yield poor histological results. Please prepare specimens with the following protocol:

Fixation protocol:

Dietrich's Fixative
    30 ml ethanol
    10 ml formalin
      2 ml glacial acetic acid
    58 ml distilled water
  100 ml total

Store fixative at room temperature. Fish should be alive before fixation.

  • Euthanize the fish with an overdose of MS-222 (tricaine, ethyl-m-aminobenzoate) or by placing them in ice water.
  • Cut off the tail posterior to the anus. Include the tail in the fixation if it appears diseased.
  • Carefully slit open the body cavity along the belly, trying not to disrupt internal organs.
  • Use 15 ml of fixative per 1-2 fish in a small vial. The volume of fixative should be 10-20 times the volume of the specimen.
  • To ensure uniform and complete fixation, fix for three days on a rotor or other agitating device.
  • Specimens may be stored in Dietrich's fixative indefinitely.

Please contact fish_health@zfin.org if you have questions about fixation or sample preparation.
