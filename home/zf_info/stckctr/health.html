Resource Center Pathology and Health Services:

The Zebrafish International Resource Center provides diagnostic services and health status testing for laboratory zebrafish. The histopathology services can be utilized to analyze specific or suspected disease problems. Diagnostic services include necropsy, bacteriology, and virology. Based upon clinical history and test results, specific recommendations for control or treatment will be provided.

The Resource Center can also provide routine sentinel or quality control testing of zebrafish from healthy laboratory colonies (e.g. to fulfill institutional animal care and use committe, IACUC, health status monitoring requirements).

Contact Jennifer Matthews, DVM, PhD, (Phone: (541) 346-6028 ext. 14 or Email: fish_health@zfin.org) for information about pathology and health services. DO NOT send fish before you have contacted Dr. Matthews to receive instructions about preparing specimens.

To analyze an outbreak of an infectious disease, 5-7 diseased fish are typically required. We provide a package rate for this service. In addition, suspected neoplasias or other problems that may involve an individual fish are very interesting to us, so please contact us even if you have only a single diseased fish.

Contact  Jennifer Matthews, DVM, PhD, (Email:fish_health@zfin.org) for information about pathology and health services.

Resource Center Health Studies:

The Resource Center Health Studies have three main aims:

1. Identify the major diseases affecting laboratory zebrafish. A major research goal of the Resource Center is to identify and characterize the diseases and health problems present in laboratory colonies of zebrafish. We encourage researchers to send us samples of diseased zebrafish. Our analysis of samples from many laboratories will provide a broad survey of most diseases affecting laboratory colonies. When a particular disease is identified, the source and course of the disease will be investigated including in vivo transmission studies.

2. Develop methods for disease control and treatment. Methods of disease control are established initially by adapting known treatments for similar diseases in other fish or animal species. The treatments are adapted using efficacy and toxicity trials.

3. Prepare a manual on zebrafish diseases. The diseases of importance to laboratory zebrafish culture are being described and illustrated. For each disease, the following is included: general overview of the disease, gross pathology and clinical signs, microscopic changes (e.g. histopathology), and control and treatment.
