Project goal

Add markers to the existing microsatellite map to an average density of 1 marker per 0.3-0.5 cM. Based on the 2000 marker map, this will require approximately 7000 total markers or 500 new markers.

Achieving our goal of mapping 5200 markers over 3 years will require mapping an average of 500 markers per quarter. This will require a team of six technicians; to date we have hired and trained three. Recruiting additional technicians is a top priority. Certain aspects of the project, particularly screening genomic libraries, are most efficient when performed by the entire team. During quarters when this work is underway, the number of markers placed on the map may be reduced.

Data release and availability

1. Sequence Tagged Sites (STS's)

STS data (genomic sequence and PCR primers) will be submitted to the dbSTS database at NCBI within 48 hours of analysis. All STS's will be submitted prior to and regardless of genotyping and mapping.

2. Microsatellite markers

New updates of the map will be calculated and released quarterly. All markers genotyped during that quarter will be included in the update. Calculating positions is a time-consuming process regardless of the number of markers being added, so it is not practical to calculate or release this data more frequently. Map locations for new markers will be added to the existing map displayed on our local web site and submitted to ZFIN once the positions have been reviewed for possible errors, typically within 48-72h of analysis.

3. Data availability

During the past quarter we have substantially enhanced our local resources for displaying map data. We have set up a central database system to consolidate data on all aspects of marker generation including sequencing, PCR primer design, genotyping and mapping. Our web site queries this database to generate up-to-date information for every request to ensure that the community has access to the latest data. In addition, map positions are submitted to ZFIN and genomic sequences to NCBI.
